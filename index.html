          <h2>About</h2>

          <p>

            I am a Lecturer in the School of Mathematical Sciences at Capital Normal University.

            Before joining Capital Normal University in October 2025, I was a postdoctoral researcher

            at the Beijing Institute of Mathematical Sciences and Applications, where I worked with

            Professors Zhengwei Liu and Jinsong Wu.

          </p >

          <p>

            I received my PhD from Purdue University in May 2023 under the supervision of

            Professor Uli Walther. I was initially trained in algebraic geometry, and my research

            interests later evolved toward mathematical physics.

          </p >

          <p>

            My work lies at the interface of mathematical physics and algebra, with particular

            interests in topological quantum field theory, tensor categories, and related structures

            arising in topological phases and lattice models.

          </p >

      <section id="research">

        <h2>Research</h2>

        <p>

          Broadly speaking, I am interested in the mathematical structures underlying topological

          phases of matter and quantum field theory. My recent work focuses on quantum double

          models, weak Hopf symmetry, tube algebras, and related categorical constructions.

        </p >

        <p>

          I also maintain a background interest in algebraic geometry, especially as part of my

          earlier mathematical training.

        </p >

      </section>
